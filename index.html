Market Concentration:

The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) is a measure of market concentration. It is calculated by summing the squares of the market shares of all firms in the market. A low HHI (<1500) indicates a competitive market, 1500-2500 indicates moderate concentration, and an HHI above 2500 indicates high concentration.

For more on the HHI calculation methodology, refer to Rhoades, S. A. (1993). "The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index." Federal Reserve Bulletin, 79(3), 188-189.
